Chicken Adobo

DESCRIPTION:

A corn dog is a classic American snack that combines a juicy hot dog with a crispy, golden-brown cornmeal batter coating. The hot dog is skewered on a stick, dipped in a thick cornmeal batter, and then deep-fried to perfection, creating a delightful contrast between the soft, meaty interior and the crunchy, savory exterior. Often served at fairs, carnivals, and sporting events, the corn dog is a beloved comfort food that captures the essence of indulgence and nostalgia, making it a quintessential part of American street food culture.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 (3 pound) chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 large onion, quartered and sliced½ pound ground pork
  • 2 tablespoons minced garlic
  • ⅔ cup low sodium soy sauce
  • ⅓ cup white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 2 teaspoons black pepper
  • 1 bay leaf

STEPS:

Step 1

Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook chicken pieces until golden brown, 2 to 3 minutes per side. Transfer chicken to a plate and set aside.

Step 2

Add onion and garlic to the skillet; cook until softened and brown, about 6 minutes.

Step 3

Add onion and garlic to the skillet; cook until softened and brown, about 6 minutes.

Step 4

Return chicken to pan, increase heat to high, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until chicken is tender and cooked through, 35 to 40 minutes.
